One in three recorded events was not a protest but an act of violence or a riot. For every march, there was, on average, half an attack. This dataset only captures what made the news.

Violence against civilians
2018–19 saw the largest spike in protests — coinciding with nationwide demonstrations against the Transgender Persons (Protection of Rights) Bill, which activists criticised for failing to protect the communities it claimed to serve.
Violence as share of all events, per year
In 2020, nearly half of all recorded events were violent — as Covid lockdowns created conditions of extreme vulnerability for transgender communities with no income or housing protection.
Where events were concentrated
Maharashtra, Kerala, and Delhi recorded the most events. Kerala's high count reflects both an active trans rights movement and documented police harassment. Jammu & Kashmir appears disproportionately — almost entirely as protests against police inaction.
